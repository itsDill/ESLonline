Practice these tricky sounds! Red = S sounds | Blue = R sounds

S Sound Practice

Practice the "S" sound at the end of words. Listen carefully and repeat!

The United States has fifty states.
France has beautiful cities and castles.
Students from many countries speak different languages.
She visits six countries this summer.

R Sound Practice

Practice the "R" sound. Your tongue should curl slightly back!

Brazil is a great country in South America.
France and Greece are in Europe.
The rivers in Africa are really long.
Travel around the world and learn different cultures.

Challenge: S + R Sounds Together!

Advanced practice! These sentences have both S and R sounds.

Several restaurants serve rice in Asian countries.
Tourists from Russia visit rair monuments.
The President speaks three languages and represents many countries.

Pronunciation Tips

S Sound Tips:
  • Put your tongue behind your top teeth
  • Let air flow through (like a snake: ssssss)
  • Practice slowly, then speed up!
R Sound Tips:
  • Curl your tongue back slightly (don't touch the roof!)
  • English R is different from many languages
  • Watch native speakers on video for help

ğŸ‘¨â€ğŸ« Teacher's Guide

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and name 10 countries around the world
  • Match countries with their capitals
  • Recognize national flags
  • Use "from" and "live in" correctly
  • Learn basic geography and cultural facts

Teaching Tips

  • Pronunciation Practice: Use the audio buttons to help students hear correct pronunciation
  • Interactive Learning: Encourage students to click all country cards to reveal information
  • Group Activity: Have students share which countries they or their families are from
  • Extension: Ask students to research one country and present 3 facts to the class
  • Assessment: Use the matching game to test comprehension

Lesson Plan (45-60 minutes)

0-10 min: Warm-up - Ask students which countries they know

10-25 min: Explore country cards together, practice pronunciation

25-35 min: Grammar focus - teach "from" and "live in"

35-50 min: Interactive games and activities

50-60 min: Review and homework assignment
