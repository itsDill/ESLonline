Everything You Need to Know About EIKEN

Category: Test Preparation — Updated: 2026-06-20

What is EIKEN?

EIKEN (The STEP EIKEN Test in Practical English Proficiency) is a popular English proficiency test in Japan that assesses practical listening, reading, writing, and speaking skills across multiple levels.

Test Levels and What They Mean

  • Grade 5 / 4: Basic vocabulary and simple sentences.
  • Grade 3: Basic communicative ability for everyday situations.
  • Pre-2 and Grade 2: Lower-intermediate to intermediate skills suitable for study and work.
  • Pre-1 and Grade 1: Upper-intermediate to advanced proficiency.

Test Format

Formats vary by level but typically include reading comprehension, listening, grammar/vocabulary, and at higher levels, writing and speaking sections. Many levels are split into a written test and a separate interview-style speaking test.

Preparation Tips

  1. Familiarize yourself with the test format for your target level.
  2. Practice timed reading and listening to build speed.
  3. Study vocabulary and collocations used in everyday English.
  4. For speaking, record short answers and compare them to model responses.
  5. Take official or high-quality practice tests to check progress.

Scoring and Results

EIKEN uses pass/fail and scaled scores depending on level. Check the official EIKEN site for exact scoring rules for the year you take the test.

Registration & Test Day

Register early through the official EIKEN website or local test centers. Bring required ID, arrive early, and follow test center instructions. For speaking tests, speak clearly and answer directly.

Resources

  • Official EIKEN website
  • Past papers and model answers
  • Local EIKEN preparation courses and tutors

Conclusion

EIKEN is a practical test that rewards steady practice across all skills. Choose the right level, practice consistently, and use targeted resources to improve.
