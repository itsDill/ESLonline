Perfect 60-Minute ESL Lesson: Social Communication

A complete lesson template covering social communication, networking vocabulary, conversation practice, and real-world scenarios for intermediate-advanced adult learners.

Duration

60 minutes

Level

Intermediate-Advanced

Focus

Social & Professional Skills

Warm-up (10 min)

📝 Key Grammar Patterns

🤝 Making Polite Requests

Would you like to join us for coffee?
Could I get you something to drink?
Would you mind if I introduced you to my colleague?

💭 Expressing Opinions

In my opinion, remote work has many advantages.
I tend to think that face-to-face meetings are more effective.
I see your point, but I have a slightly different perspective.

🎯 Practice Activities

Role-play: Networking Event

Students practice introducing themselves and making small talk at a simulated networking event. Focus on using the vocabulary and grammar patterns learned.

Conversation Starters

Practice opening conversations with different types of people in various professional and social settings. Include follow-up questions and active listening.

Small Talk Scenarios

Engage in realistic small talk situations: waiting for a meeting, coffee break conversations, elevator encounters, and casual workplace interactions.

Professional Introductions

Learn to introduce yourself professionally, exchange business cards appropriately, and remember important details about the people you meet.

💬 Sample Conversations

Networking Event Introduction

Person A: "Hi, I'm Sarah. I work in marketing for a tech startup."

Person B: "Nice to meet you, Sarah. I'm Mike from ABC Consulting. How are you finding the event?"

Person A: "It's great! Lots of interesting people. What kind of consulting do you do?"
Person B: "We help companies with digital transformation. What's your startup focused on?"

Person A: "We're developing AI solutions for small businesses. Always looking for implementation partners!"

Person B: "That sounds fascinating. Here's my card - I'd love to learn more."

📋 Teacher Notes & Tips

🎯 Lesson Objectives

  • Use social vocabulary in context
  • Practice polite conversation patterns
  • Build confidence in networking situations
  • Develop active listening skills

💡 Teaching Tips

  • Encourage students to share personal experiences
  • Focus on natural, authentic interactions
  • Provide positive feedback on attempts
  • Allow time for reflection and questions

📝 Homework Ideas

  • Practice one new conversation starter daily
  • Attend a local networking event or meetup
  • Write a personal elevator pitch
  • Keep a vocabulary journal with new terms
