🎯 Unit 1: To Be + Possessive Adjectives

The Verb "To Be" - Form

Positive: I am / You are / He/She/It is / We are / They are

Negative: I am not / You are not / He/She/It is not / We are not / They are not

Questions: Am I? / Are you? / Is he/she/it? / Are we? / Are they?

Form Example
Positive I am a receptionist. She is from Brazil.
Negative I am not a manager. He isn't Japanese.
Questions Are you Italian? Is she a technician?
Short Answers Yes, I am. / No, he isn't. / Yes, they are.
Real Business Examples:

✓ "Hello, I'm Gundula. I'm a Financial Director."

✓ "Is he from Poland?" "Yes, he is."

✓ "They aren't technicians. They're team leaders."

💡 Quick Tip:

Remember short forms: I'm, you're, he's, she's, it's, we're, they're

Also: I'm not, he isn't, they aren't

Possessive Adjectives

Use possessive adjectives to show who owns or has something.

Subject Pronoun Possessive Adjective Example
I my My name is Haruo.
you your Your desk is here.
he his His company is in Sweden.
she her Her assistant is Pauline.
it its Its head office is in London.
we our Our team is very small.
they their Their office is in Cairo.

✏️ Exercise 1.1: Complete with the correct form of "to be"

1. She ____ a Financial Director.

Answer: is ("She is a Financial Director.")

2. They ____ from Poland. They ____ Polish.

Answer: are, are ("They are from Poland. They are Polish.")

3. ____ you from Italy? No, I ____ not. I ____ from Spain.

Answer: Are, am, am ("Are you from Italy? No, I am not. I am from Spain.")

4. The product ____ American, but it ____ not British.

Answer: is, is ("The product is American, but it is not British.")

✏️ Exercise 1.2: Choose the correct possessive adjective

1. This is my department. Lawrie is my / his boss.

Answer: my

2. She has an assistant. Her / His name is Pauline.

Answer: Her

3. You are next to Harald. Those are your / his biscuits on his desk.

Answer: his

4. The coffee machine is for our / their office only.

Answer: our
⚡ Unit 2: Present Simple

Present Simple - Form

Positive: Subject + verb (+ -s/es for he/she/it)

Negative: Subject + don't/doesn't + verb

Questions: Do/Does + subject + verb?

Form Example Note
Positive I work for IBM. She works for Google. Add -s or -es for 3rd person (he/she/it)
Negative I don't work for Apple. He doesn't export to France. Main verb = base form (no -s)
Questions Do you work here? Does she provide training? Main verb = base form
Short Answers Yes, I do. / No, she doesn't.
💡 Spelling Rules for 3rd Person Singular (-s/-es):

✓ Most verbs: add -s → work → works, sell → sells

✓ Verbs ending in -ch, -ss, -sh, -x: add -es → watch → watches, miss → misses

✓ Consonant + -y: change to -ies → try → tries, apply → applies

✓ Exceptions: have → has, go → goes

Real Business Examples:

✓ "We employ 6,000 people worldwide."

✓ "The company doesn't export to Asia."

✓ "Does Samsung produce electronics?" "Yes, it does."

✓ "What do they provide?" "They provide software training."

✏️ Exercise 2.1: Form the correct questions and short answers

1. ____ you export your products to France?

Answer: Do ("Do you export your products to France?" / "Yes, we do.")

2. ____ they work for General Motors? Yes, they ____.

Answer: Do, do ("Do they work for General Motors? Yes, they do.")

3. ____ she provide training? No, she ____.

Answer: Does, doesn't ("Does she provide training? No, she doesn't.")

✏️ Exercise 2.2: Fill in the correct form of the verb

1. The company ____ (produce) computer components.

Answer: produces

2. It ____ (employ) 800 people in Europe.

Answer: employs

3. We ____ (not export) to America, but we ____ (export) to Asia.

Answer: don't export, do export ("We don't export to America, but we do export to Asia.")

4. She ____ (have) two assistants, and they ____ (work) in the office.

Answer: has, work ("She has two assistants, and they work in the office.")
🏢 Unit 3: There is/are + Some/Any

There is / There are

Positive: There is (singular) / There are (plural)

Negative: There isn't / There aren't

Questions: Is there? / Are there?

Short Answers: Yes, there is/are. / No, there isn't/aren't.

Form Singular Plural
Positive There's a factory in Beijing. There are offices worldwide.
Negative There isn't an office in Africa. There aren't any shops here.
Questions Is there a bank nearby? Are there parking spaces?
Short Answers Yes, there is. / No, there isn't. Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.

Some / Any

Some: Use in positive sentences with plural nouns

Any: Use in negative sentences and questions

Some Examples:

✓ "There are some products in the warehouse."

✓ "Are there any managers available?" (Question)

✓ "There aren't any offices in Dubai." (Negative)

💡 Remember:

• Use some in positive sentences

• Use any in negative sentences and questions

• Use a or an with singular nouns

• Don't forget the subject "there" in questions: "Is there...?" not "There is...?"

✏️ Exercise 3.1: There is/are questions and answers

1. ____ ____ a restaurant near the office? Yes, ____ ____.

Answer: Is there, there is

2. ____ ____ parking spaces in the car park? Yes, ____ ____.

Answer: Are there, there are

3. ____ ____ a bank in the conference centre? No, ____ ____ ____ bank there.

Answer: Is there, there isn't a (or "there is no")

4. ____ ____ meeting rooms? Yes, ____ ____ three large meeting rooms.

Answer: Are there, there are

✏️ Exercise 3.2: Choose Some or Any

1. There are some / any restaurants in the conference centre.

Answer: some (Positive sentence)

2. Are there some / any shops nearby?

Answer: any (Question)

3. There aren't some / any sales reps in China.

Answer: any (Negative sentence)

4. We need some / any name cards for the meeting.

Answer: some (Positive statement - we need them)
⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Mistake: Wrong verb with "there"

Wrong: "There is five people."

Correct: "There are five people."

Remember: Use "is" with singular, "are" with plural

❌ Mistake: Using -s in negative questions

Wrong: "Does she works for Google?"

Correct: "Does she work for Google?"

Remember: Base form after "does/do"

❌ Mistake: "Some" in questions

Wrong: "Are there some restaurants here?"

Correct: "Are there any restaurants here?"

Remember: Use "any" in questions, "some" in positive

❌ Mistake: Wrong possessive

Wrong: "She have an assistant."

Correct: "She has an assistant."

Remember: he/she/it + has (exception)

❌ Mistake: Forgetting -es ending

Wrong: "He watch the presentation."

Correct: "He watches the presentation."

Remember: -ch, -ss, -sh, -x → add -es

❌ Mistake: Short answer form

Wrong: "Do you work here?" "Yes, I work."

Correct: "Do you work here?" "Yes, I do."

Remember: Use the auxiliary verb in short answers

💬 Real Business Dialogues

📞 Dialogue 1: First Meeting

A: Hello. I'm Sophia. I'm a Financial Director at Techno Solutions.

B: Pleased to meet you, Sophia. I'm James. Are you from the UK?

A: No, I'm not. I'm from Portugal. How are you?

B: I'm fine, thanks. Do you work in London?

A: Yes, I do. Our head office is here. There are 200 people in our office. Where's your company?

B: We're in Manchester. There are 50 people in our team.

Grammar focus: Use "to be" for introductions, present simple for facts, "there is/are" for locations

📞 Dialogue 2: Company Information

A: What does your company produce?

B: We produce software. Do you work in IT?

A: No, I don't. I work in finance. Is there a restaurant near your office?

B: Yes, there is. There are three restaurants nearby.

A: That's great! Are there any parking spaces?

B: Yes, there are plenty of parking spaces. We have 100 spaces for our employees.

Grammar focus: Present simple questions, "there is/are", possessive adjectives

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Unit 1: Use "to be" to describe who you are and where you're from. Use possessive adjectives (my, your, his, her, etc.) to show ownership.
  • Unit 2: Present simple describes what you do regularly or general facts. Remember to add -s/-es for he/she/it in positive sentences, but NOT in negative or questions.
  • Unit 3: Use "There is/are" to talk about what exists. Use "some" in positive sentences and "any" in negative/questions.
  • Practice Tip: Record yourself speaking these sentences out loud. Hearing yourself helps grammar become natural!
