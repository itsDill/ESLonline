Cultural Considerations

üá¨üáß

British English

British culture values understatement and indirect communication. Common phrases include "quite," "rather," and "I'm afraid." Example: "I'm afraid that might not work" instead of "That won't work."

üá∫üá∏

American English

American communication tends to be more direct while still polite. It's common to use "please" and "thank you" frequently, and to be enthusiastic in responses. Example: "That would be great!" or "I'd really appreciate that."

üåè

International English

When speaking with non-native speakers, use clear, simple language. Avoid idioms, slang, and cultural references. Speak at a moderate pace and be patient with comprehension.
