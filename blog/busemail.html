The Professional Email Foundation

The Professional Email Trinity
1. Appropriate Tone - Match formality to relationship
2. Clear Purpose - State your objective upfront
3. Action-Oriented Close - What happens next?
Universal Professional Email Template
Subject: [Specific + Action] e.g., "Meeting Request: Q4 Strategy Discussion"
Greeting: Dear [Name] / Hello [Name]
Opening: Context in 1-2 sentences
Body: Main request/information (3-4 sentences max)
Close: Clear next step + timeline
Sign-off: Best regards, [Name]

Email Checklists

Before Sending Checklist
  • Clear, specific subject line
  • Recipient's name spelled correctly
  • Purpose stated in first sentence
  • One main objective per email
  • Clear call-to-action
  • Professional greeting and closing
  • Spell-checked and proofread
  • Attachments mentioned and attached
  • Appropriate tone for relationship
  • Mobile-friendly formatting
High-Priority Email Checklist
  • Mark as urgent only if truly urgent
  • Include deadline in subject line
  • State urgency reason clearly
  • Provide context for time-sensitivity
  • Offer alternative solutions
  • Include your contact information
  • Send to decision-makers only
  • Follow up with phone call if needed
  • Thank recipient for quick response
  • Document response for future reference
Client Communication Checklist
  • Use formal greeting (Dear Mr./Ms.)
  • Reference previous interactions
  • Professional email signature
  • Company branding if applicable
  • Clear next steps outlined
  • Timeline expectations set
  • Contact information provided
  • Professional closing statement
  • No casual language or slang
  • Backup contact if you're unavailable
Team/Internal Email Checklist
  • Relevant team members included
  • Project/task clearly identified
  • Action items bullet-pointed
  • Deadlines specified
  • Resources/links provided
  • Meeting details if applicable
  • Questions clearly separated
  • Priority level indicated
  • CC/BCC used appropriately
  • Follow-up timeline mentioned
