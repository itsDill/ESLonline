‚Ä¢ Think of a growling lion: "Rrrr!"
‚Ä¢ Practice words: red, run, car, star, rabbit

‚Ä¢ Practice words: sun, snake, bus, yes, bicycle

Word Detective Game

Give kids a mystery word with 3 clues. They have to guess the word and then use it in their own sentence!

Example: "I'm thinking of something that... 1) You sleep in it, 2) It has sheets and pillows, 3) It's in your bedroom." (Answer: bed)
Color & Describe Challenge

Kids pick an object and describe it using colors, shapes, sizes, and feelings. Great for building descriptive vocabulary!

Example: "My teddy bear is brown and fluffy. It's small and soft. It makes me feel happy and safe!"

Super Fun Activities & Games

Question Chain Game

Students take turns asking and answering questions, then asking a new question to the next person. Great for building natural conversation flow!

Example: "What's your favorite animal?" ‚Üí "I like cats. What about you, do you have a pet?"
Accent Charades

Kids act out different scenarios while trying to use different accents. Super fun and removes pressure from perfect pronunciation!

Scenarios: Ordering ice cream, meeting a new friend, talking about pets, describing favorite toys
Mini Interviews

Students interview each other about simple topics, practicing question formation and natural responses.

Topics: Favorite foods, hobbies, family, school subjects, cartoon characters
Story Building Circle

Each student adds one sentence to create a group story. Encourages creativity and listening skills while practicing different sentence structures.

Starter: "Once upon a time, there was a talking cat who loved to bake cookies..."
Scavenger Hunt Conversations

Kids walk around and find classmates who match certain criteria, then have mini-conversations about their discoveries!

Find someone who: Has a pet, likes pizza, can swim, has been to another country, speaks two languages
Compliment Circle

Students practice giving and receiving compliments using different sentence structures. Builds confidence and positive classroom culture!

Examples: "I like your...", "You are really good at...", "Thank you for..."
Express Yourself Challenge

Quick 30-second challenges where kids describe pictures, explain how to do something, or share opinions on fun topics.

Topics: Describe your dream bedroom, explain how to make a sandwich, what's the best superpower and why?
