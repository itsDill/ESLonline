Learning Tips for Social Vocabulary

💡 Practice Strategy

Use these words in context during real conversations. Start with one new word per day and practice using it in different situations.

🎯 Memory Technique

Create personal associations with each word. Think of specific situations where you might use each term.

📝 Active Usage

Write example sentences with each new word. Practice role-playing scenarios with friends or colleagues.

📝 Social Grammar Patterns

🤝 Making Polite Requests & Offers

Use these patterns to be polite and professional in social situations.

Would you like to join us for coffee after the meeting?
Could I get you something to drink?
Would you mind if I introduced you to my colleague?

Complete the polite requests:

___ to exchange business cards?

___ tell me more about your work?

___ sharing your contact information?

💭 Expressing Opinions & Agreement

Learn to share your thoughts and respond to others diplomatically.

In my opinion, remote work has many advantages.
I tend to think that face-to-face meetings are more effective.
I see your point, but I have a slightly different perspective.

Express opinions diplomatically:

___ teamwork is essential for success.

___ networking events are very valuable.

___ we should also consider other options.

💬 Small Talk Flow Simulator

Follow the conversation flow and practice natural small talk patterns.

1
Greeting & Opening: "Hi! How are you enjoying the event?"
2
Find Common Ground: "Are you familiar with this industry?"
3
Share Information: "I work in marketing. What about you?"
4
Ask Follow-up: "That sounds interesting! How did you get started?"
5
Closing: "It was great meeting you! Here's my card."
