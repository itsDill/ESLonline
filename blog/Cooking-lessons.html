📚 Grammar Focus: Imperatives & Sequence Markers

Imperatives: Commands that tell someone what to do

Examples: "Peel the potatoes." "Stir the sauce." "Bake for 20 minutes."

Sequence Markers: Words that show order and steps

Examples: First, Then, Next, After that, Finally

🌍 Cultural Differences in Cooking

Notice how different cultures use these same cooking methods for different dishes. For example, Italians bake pasta in casseroles, while Asians often stir-fry noodles. Same techniques, different traditions!

Key Language Patterns:

Imperatives for Instructions:

• Use base form of verb: "Chop the onions" (not "chopping")

• Be direct and clear: "Pour the milk" (not "you should pour")

Sequence Markers:

• First/Initially → Then/Next → After that → Finally/Lastly

Speaking Practice: Now you can show understanding by giving cooking instructions to a partner using imperatives and sequence markers!

🌏 Cultural Connections

Different cultures have unique ways of preparing similar ingredients. Italian pasta, Asian noodles, and Mexican tortillas all use similar basic cooking techniques but create completely different dishes!
