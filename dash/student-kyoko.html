💡 Speaking Tips for Kyoko
Key Focus Areas for Improvement
🎯 Extend your answers: Don't just give one-word responses. Add reasons, examples, or follow-up thoughts. "Yes, I like it because..." instead of just "Yes."
⏸️ Use fillers naturally: "Well...", "Let me think...", "That's a good question..." — these buy thinking time and sound natural.
🔗 Connect your ideas: Use linking words like "however," "on the other hand," "for instance," to make your speech flow better.
🎭 Show emotion: Vary your intonation. Express surprise, enthusiasm, or concern to make conversations more engaging.
❓ Ask follow-up questions: Good conversations are two-way. Practice asking "What about you?" or "How do you feel about that?"
🔄 Paraphrase when stuck: If you don't know a word, describe it. "The thing you use to..." is better than silence.

✍️ Task 2 Essay — PEEL Structure

P
Point
State your main argument clearly. One idea per paragraph. "One key reason is that..."
E
Explain
Develop and explain the idea. "This means that..." / "In other words..."
E
Evidence
Give a real example, statistic, or case. "For instance, in Japan..."
L
Link
Tie back to the question. "This demonstrates that..." / "Therefore..."

🎤 Speaking Part 2 — STAR Technique

You have 1 minute to prepare and 2 minutes to speak on a topic card. Use STAR to structure your talk:

S — Situation
Set the scene. When, where, who? ("I remember when I was studying for...")
T — Task
What was the challenge or goal? ("I needed to...")
A — Action
What did you do? ("So I decided to... because...")
R — Result
What happened? How did you feel? ("As a result, I learned...")

📖 Reading — True / False / Not Given Strategy

Answer What it means Key signal
TRUE The text confirms the statement Same meaning, possibly different words
FALSE The text contradicts the statement Text says the opposite
NOT GIVEN No information about this in the text Topic may appear but statement is NOT addressed
⚠️ Most common mistake: Using your own knowledge to answer. Only use what is written in the passage. If you can't find it — it's NOT GIVEN.

📄 Personal Statement

  • Open with a specific experience, not a generic statement
  • Explain why you chose your subject — with real reasons
  • Show academic interest: books read, topics explored
  • Mention extracurriculars that show relevant skills
  • End with future goals: how will this degree help you?

🎤 University Interview

  • Prepare for subject-specific questions — read ahead
  • Use the STAR method for experience-based questions
  • Ask the interviewer questions at the end — show curiosity
  • It's OK to pause: "That's a great question, let me think..."
  • Be honest — interviewers prefer genuine to perfect answers

✍️ Writing Practice & Templates

📄 IELTS Task 2 — Essay Template (250 words)

Paragraph 1 — Introduction (40–50 words)
Paraphrase the question. State your position clearly.
"It is widely argued that [topic]. While some believe [view A], I would argue that [your position] because [brief reason]."
Paragraph 2 — Body 1: Main Argument (70–80 words)
Point → Explain → Evidence → Link (PEEL)
"One key reason is that [P]. This means that [E]. For example, [E]. Therefore, [L]."
Paragraph 3 — Body 2: Second Argument or Counterargument (70–80 words)
Address the other side or give a second supporting reason.
"Although some people argue that [counterargument], I believe [your view] because [reason]."
Paragraph 4 — Conclusion (30–40 words)
Summarise your argument. Do NOT introduce new ideas.
"In conclusion, I maintain that [position]. While [acknowledge counterargument], the evidence suggests that [final view]."

🎌 Eiken Grade 1 Essay Template (200 words)

Opening: State your position (25–30 words)
"I believe that [position]. There are two main reasons for this view."
Reason 1 + Example (60–70 words)
"First, [reason 1]. For instance, [specific example]. This shows that [link to your argument]."
Reason 2 + Example (60–70 words)
"Second, [reason 2]. A good example of this is [example]. Therefore, [conclusion for this point]."
Conclusion (25–30 words)
"For these reasons, I believe that [restate position]. [Optional: acknowledge the other view briefly]."
