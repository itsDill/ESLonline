⭐ Focus: go / went / gone (and key irregular verbs) ▼
go → went → gone is one of the most common irregular verbs in English. The three forms are: base form (present) → past simple → past participle (used with have/has).
Tense Form used Example
Present Simple go I go to work every day.
Past Simple went I went to Singapore last year.
Present Perfect have/has + gone / been I have been to three countries.
(gone = left and not back; been = visited)
⚠️ Trick question! "I have been to Paris" = I visited Paris (experience).
"He has gone to Paris" = He is there now (not back yet).
For life experience questions, use have been to.
Q: I ___ to three different countries for work. (life experience) → have been
Q: We ___ to the trade fair in Singapore. (past, specific time) → went

📐 12 Grammar Points — Quick Cards ▼

G1 · Present Simple: BE Q1

I am · He/She/It is · You/We/They are
"She is the CEO." / "They are partners."

G2 · Present Simple: Other Verbs Q3, Q4

He/She/It → add -s · Negative: doesn't + base
"She doesn't manage the team." / "It exports."

G3 · There is / There are Q5

Singular → Is there? / Plural → Are there?
"Is there a cafeteria?" / "Are there any rooms?"

G4 · Adverbs of Frequency Q7

After be: "He is always late." · Before main verb: "I always check."
always · usually · often · sometimes · rarely · never

G5 · Past Simple: BE Q9

I/He/She/It → was · You/We/They → were
"The presentation was impressive." / "Were they there?"

G6 · Past Simple: Regular Q10, Q26, Q29

Base + -ed: worked, signed, achieved, launched
"She signed the contract last Tuesday." / "They achieved great results."

G7 · Past Simple: Irregular Q11, Q25

go→went · speak→spoke · have→had
"I spoke to the MD at the conference." / "We went to Singapore."

G8 · Prepositions of Movement Q6, Q13

along a corridor · past a place · on a floor
"Go along the corridor." / "HR is on the second floor."

G9 · Present Continuous Q15

am/is/are + verb-ing
"She is presenting to the board right now."

G10 · Comparatives & Superlatives Q17, Q18, Q27

Short: -er / -est · Long: more / the most · good → better / the best
"More reliable than…" / "The most innovative product."

G11 · Going to Q21, Q28

I am going to + base verb (planned decision)
"I am going to take the early flight."

G12 · Present Perfect Q16, Q23, Q24, Q25

have/has + past participle
"She has worked here for five years." / "Have you ever worked abroad?"
🔁 Present Perfect vs Past Simple — Know the difference! ▼
This is the most tested grammar area in the test. Many students confuse these two tenses. Learn the key signals!
Present Perfect (have + V3) Past Simple (V2 or -ed)
Used for experience (ever/never) Used for a specific past time
Used with: ever, never, already, yet, just, since, for, recently Used with: yesterday, last week, in 2022, ago, on Tuesday
"I have worked here for five years." "I worked in marketing in 2019."
"Have you ever worked overseas?" "Did you go to the conference?"
"She hasn't finished the report yet." "She didn't finish the report yesterday."
"I have been to three countries." (life exp.) "I went to Singapore last year." (specific)
⚠️ Test questions that use this:
Q16: "I have worked in marketing for five years." ✅ (experience on CV)
Q23: "Have you ever worked in a foreign country?" ✅
Q24: "She hasn't finished the report yet." ✅
Q25: "I have been to three countries." ✅ (life experience)
Q26: "We signed the deal in March 2022." ✅ (specific past date)

💡 Test Tips — How to approach each question ▼

🕵️ Fill-in-the-blank Grammar

  • Identify the tense signal word in the sentence (last year → past simple; ever/yet → present perfect; right now → present continuous; going to → future plan).
  • Check the subject (he/she/it → use -s or was, not were).

🗣 Professional Language

  • For "most professional" questions, look for formal, polite language.
  • Avoid options that sound rude, casual, or strange in a business context.

📊 Comparative vs Superlative

  • Comparing two things: use comparative (-er / more…)
  • Best/worst in a group: use superlative (the -est / the most…)
  • Irregular: good → better → the best

⏳ Tense Time Signals

  • last year / in 2022 / yesterday → Past Simple
  • ever / never / yet / already / since / for → Present Perfect
  • right now / at the moment → Present Continuous
  • going to / planning to → Future plan

🎤 Speaking Tips

  • Use the language tips printed on each task card.
  • Mix tenses: talk about the past (past simple), experience (present perfect), and future plans (going to).
  • It's OK to pause and think — speak clearly, not fast.
