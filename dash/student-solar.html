  • 🔤
    Simple Present Tense "I play games / He plays games"
    Grammar ▼

    📘 Explanation

    We use the simple present to talk about things that happen every day, all the time, or are always true.

    For he / she / it — add -s or -es to the verb.

    ✅ I play video games every day. ✅ He plays with his robot after school. ✅ She likes pizza. ❌ He play video games. (wrong!)

    🎯 Practice Activities

    • Fill in "I ___ (like) robots. He ___ (like) dinosaurs."
    • Say it Tell me 3 things you do every day using "I + verb"
    • Game Verb Tennis — take turns: "I eat" → "He eats" → "I run" → "He runs"
    • Make it Make sentences about your family: "My mom ___. My dad ___."
    • Tip Remember: do NOT add -s for I, you, we, they!
  • ⏳
    Present Continuous "I am playing right now!"
    Grammar ▼

    📘 Explanation

    We use the present continuous to talk about what is happening right now, at this moment.

    Formula: I am / He is / They are + verb-ing

    ✅ I am playing a game right now. ✅ He is building a robot. ✅ We are eating lunch. ❌ I playing a game. (missing "am"!)

    🎯 Practice Activities

    • Fill in "She ___ (is/am) reading. They ___ (is/are) running."
    • Game Act It Out — teacher mimes an action, student says "You are ___-ing!"
    • Look Look around the room — what is happening? "The fan is spinning."
    • Draw Draw a picture, then describe it: "The boy is jumping."
    • Tip Present continuous = happening NOW. Simple present = happens every day.
  • ❓
    Questions with Do / Does "Do you like robots?" "Does he play?"
    Grammar ▼

    📘 Explanation

    To ask a yes/no question in simple present, put Do or Does at the start.

    Do → I, you, we, they  |  Does → he, she, it

    After "does", the verb goes back to normal (no -s).

    ✅ Do you like robots? ✅ Does he play Minecraft? ✅ Do they have a computer? ❌ Does he plays? (no -s after does!)

    🎯 Practice Activities

    • Ask me Ask your teacher 5 questions using "Do you…?"
    • Game Question Ping Pong — take turns asking Do/Does questions
    • Fix it "Does she likes pizza?" → What's wrong? Fix it!
    • Interview Interview a friend: "Do you have a pet? Does your mom cook?"
    • Tip Short answers: "Yes, I do." / "No, he doesn't."
  • 👆
    This / That / These / Those "This is my robot. Those are stars."
    Grammar ▼

    📘 Explanation

    This = 1 thing, close to you  |  That = 1 thing, far away

    These = many things, close  |  Those = many things, far away

    ✅ This is my pencil. (holding it) ✅ That is your bag. (over there) ✅ These are my toys. (right here) ✅ Those are clouds. (up in the sky)

    🎯 Practice Activities

    • Game Point & Say — point at things near and far, use this/that/these/those
    • Choose "___ is a pen" (this or that?) — hold it up or put it far away
    • Draw Draw a near scene and far scene, label with this/these and that/those
    • Sort Put words in the right box: near + one, near + many, far + one, far + many
    • Tip Think: close = this/these, far = that/those. One = this/that, many = these/those.
  • 🔢
    Countable & Uncountable "How many robots?" / "How much water?"
    Grammar ▼

    📘 Explanation

    Countable = you can count them: 1 robot, 2 robots, 3 robots.

    Uncountable = you can't count them: water, rice, money, homework.

    Use "How many" for countable. Use "How much" for uncountable.

    ✅ How many games do you have? ✅ How much water do you want? ❌ How many water? (water is uncountable!) ❌ How much robots? (robots are countable!)

    🎯 Practice Activities

    • Game Sort It! — word cards into "countable" and "uncountable" piles
    • Fill in "How ___ (many/much) homework do you have?"
    • Kitchen Look at food: "Rice — uncountable. Apples — countable. Juice — uncountable."
    • Ask Ask your teacher: "How many/much ___ do you have?"
    • Tip Can you say "one" or "two" of it? Yes → countable. No → uncountable.
  • 📍
    Prepositions of Place in, on, under, next to, between
    Grammar ▼

    📘 Explanation

    Prepositions tell us where something is.

    in = inside  |  on = on top  |  under = below  |  next to = beside  |  between = in the middle of two things

    ✅ The robot is on the table. ✅ My phone is in my bag. ✅ The cat is under the chair. ✅ The book is between the pen and the eraser.

    🎯 Practice Activities

    • Game Hide & Seek Object — hide a toy: "Where is it?" → "It's under the book!"
    • Describe Look at your desk — where is everything? Make 5 sentences.
    • Game Simon Says with prepositions: "Put the pen ON your head!"
    • Fix it "The ball is at the box" → wrong! What's the right preposition?
    • Draw Draw a room and describe where things are: "The lamp is next to the bed."
  • 💪
    Can / Can't (Ability) "I can run fast!" "Robots can't eat."
    Grammar ▼

    📘 Explanation

    Can = I'm able to do it. Can't = I'm not able to do it.

    After "can" the verb stays normal (no -s, no -ing).

    Questions: Can you swim? → "Yes, I can." / "No, I can't."

    ✅ I can ride a bike. ✅ She can't fly. ✅ Can robots talk? Yes, some can! ❌ I can swimming. (just "swim"!)

    🎯 Practice Activities

    • List Write 5 things you CAN do and 5 things you CAN'T do
    • Game Can You…? Challenge — "Can you touch your nose? Can you say 'supercalifragilistic'?"
    • Compare "A fish can swim but can't walk. A bird can fly but can't…"
    • Game Superhero Game — invent a superhero: "He can fly. He can't be hurt."
    • Tip Can is the same for everyone: I can, he can, she can, they can.
  • 🎨
    Adjectives big, small, fast, slow, cool, scary
    Grammar ▼

    📘 Explanation

    Adjectives are describing words. They tell us what something looks like, feels like, or is like.

    Adjectives go before the noun: "a big robot" or after "is": "The robot is big".

    ✅ That's a cool game! ✅ The dinosaur is scary. ✅ I have a fast computer. ✅ This pizza is hot and delicious!

    🎯 Practice Activities

    • Game Describe the Monster — draw a monster and describe it: "It has big eyes and small ears."
    • Opposites Match the opposites: big↔small, fast↔slow, hot↔cold, happy↔sad
    • 5 Senses Describe your lunch: how does it look? taste? smell?
    • Fill in "I have a ___ dog. It is ___. My friend has a ___ cat."
    • Game Adjective Race — who can say more adjectives for "a robot" in 30 seconds?

    "What's your favorite game?" Talking about games & giving reasons
    Talk ▼

    💡 Starter Questions

    • What game do you play the most?
    • Why do you like it? Is it fun? Hard? Exciting?
    • Who do you play with? Friends? Family? Online?
    • What's the best thing about that game?

    🗣️ Useful Phrases

    "My favorite game is ___ because it's ___." "I like it because you can ___." "I play it every day / on weekends." "It's more fun than ___."

    🎯 Activities

    • Role Play Pretend you're a game reviewer — describe a game to your teacher
    • Game Guess My Game — describe a game without saying its name!
    • Compare "Minecraft is ___ but Roblox is ___. I prefer ___ because ___."
  • 🤖
    "If you could build a robot…" Describing inventions & imagining
    Talk ▼

    💡 Starter Questions

    • If you could build any robot, what would it do?
    • What would it look like? Big? Small? What color?
    • Would it help you? How?
    • What would you name your robot?

    🗣️ Useful Phrases

    "My robot would be ___ and it can ___." "It would help me with ___." "It has ___ arms and ___ eyes." "I would call it ___."

    🎯 Activities

    • Draw Draw your dream robot and present it to your teacher in English
    • Game Robot Show — pretend to be your robot and describe what you can do
    • Write Write 5 sentences about your robot using "It can…" and "It has…"
  • 🚀
    "Would you go to space?" Simple opinions & reasons why
    Talk ▼

    💡 Starter Questions

    • Would you go to space? Why or why not?
    • Which planet would you visit?
    • What would you take with you?
    • Are you scared of space? What's cool about it?

    🗣️ Useful Phrases

    "I would go to ___ because ___." "I think space is ___." (cool / scary / amazing) "I would take ___ with me." "I want to see ___."

    🎯 Activities

    • Game Planet Picker — pick a planet card and say 3 things about it
    • Imagine "I'm an astronaut. Today I went to Mars and I saw…"
    • Vocab Learn space words: planet, star, moon, rocket, astronaut, alien
  • 🏫
    "Tell me about your day" Daily routines, school, activities
    Talk ▼

    💡 Starter Questions

    • What time do you wake up?
    • What do you do before school?
    • What's your favorite subject?
    • What do you do after school?

    🗣️ Useful Phrases

    "First, I ___. Then I ___. After that, I ___." "I wake up at ___ o'clock." "My favorite subject is ___ because ___." "After school, I usually ___."

    🎯 Activities

    • Timeline Draw your daily timeline with pictures, present it in English
    • Game True or False — say things about your day, teacher guesses: true or false?
    • Compare "On weekdays I ___. On weekends I ___."
  • 🍕
    "What do you want for lunch?" Food, likes & dislikes, ordering
    Talk ▼

    💡 Starter Questions

    • What's your favorite food?
    • Do you like spicy food? Sweet food?
    • What food do you NOT like?
    • Can you cook anything?

    🗣️ Useful Phrases

    "I love ___ because it's ___." "I don't like ___ because it's too ___." "Can I have ___, please?" "I want ___ for lunch/dinner."

    🎯 Activities

    • Game Restaurant Role Play — one person is the waiter, one orders food
    • Menu Design your dream restaurant menu in English
    • Top 5 Make a Top 5 favorite foods list and explain each one
  • 🦖
    "What animal is the coolest?" Comparing animals & describing
    Talk ▼

    💡 Starter Questions

    • What's the coolest animal? Why?
    • If you could be any animal, which one?
    • Which is faster, a cheetah or a horse?
    • Do you have a pet? What kind?

    🗣️ Useful Phrases

    "I think ___ is the coolest because ___." "A ___ is bigger/faster/stronger than a ___." "If I could be an animal, I would be a ___." "___ can ___ but ___ can't."

    🎯 Activities

    • Game Animal Quiz — describe an animal, the other person guesses
    • Compare Use "bigger, smaller, faster" to compare two animals
    • Invent Invent a new animal — what does it look like? What can it do?
  • 🖥️
    "How does a computer work?" Simple tech explanations
    Talk ▼

    💡 Starter Questions

    • What parts does a computer have?
    • What do you use a computer for?
    • Do you prefer a tablet, phone, or computer?
    • What's inside a computer?

    🗣️ Useful Phrases

    "A computer has a screen, keyboard, and mouse." "I use a computer to ___." (play / study / watch) "The ___ is for ___-ing." (screen → looking, keyboard → typing) "I think ___ is better because ___."

    🎯 Activities

    • Label Draw a computer and label the parts in English
    • Game Tech Teacher — explain how to use a mouse to someone who has never seen one
    • Vocab Learn: screen, keyboard, mouse, click, type, download, internet
  • 🌍
    "Where do you want to go?" Countries, travel dreams, "I want to…"
    Talk ▼

    💡 Starter Questions

    • Which country do you want to visit?
    • Have you been to another country before?
    • What would you do there?
    • Do you want to go by plane, train, or boat?

    🗣️ Useful Phrases

    "I want to go to ___ because ___." "I would eat ___ and see ___." "I want to travel by ___." "___ is famous for ___."

    🎯 Activities

    • Postcard Write a postcard from your dream country: "Hi! I'm in ___. Today I…"
    • Game Where Am I? — describe a country and the other person guesses
    • Plan Plan a trip: where, how, what to eat, what to see — present it!

📝 Teacher Notes
🌟 Keep it fun and moving fast!
  • Solar is 8 years old — short attention span, so keep activities to 5–10 minutes each
  • He loves tech, robots, video games — use these as hooks for every grammar and conversation topic
  • Keep explanations super simple with lots of examples he can relate to
  • Praise effort and celebrate small wins — high fives, "great job!", stickers
  • Mix grammar drills with game-style activities to hold attention
  • If energy drops, switch to a quick game or a fun "would you rather" question
  • Use visuals, drawings, and gestures — avoid long text-based explanations
  • Let him move around or act things out when possible — kinesthetic learning helps at this age
