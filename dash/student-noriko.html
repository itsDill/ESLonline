🎯 Common Everyday Expressions (20)

Essential phrases for daily conversations

1. What's up? ›
Meaning: How are you? / What's happening?
Response: "Not much, you?" or "Just chilling!"
Usage: Super casual greeting
2. No worries ›
Meaning: It's okay, don't worry about it
Example: "Thanks!" - "No worries!"
Usage: Casual response to thanks or apologies
3. I'm down ›
Meaning: I agree, I'm interested
Example: "Want to grab lunch?" - "I'm down!"
Usage: Agreeing to plans
4. For real? ›
Meaning: Really? Are you serious?
Example: "I won the lottery!" - "For real?"
Usage: Expressing surprise or disbelief
5. My bad ›
Meaning: My mistake, I'm sorry
Example: "My bad, I forgot to call you."
Usage: Casual apology
6. Catch you later ›
Meaning: See you later, goodbye
Example: "Gotta run - catch you later!"
Usage: Casual goodbye
7. That's cool ›
Meaning: That's good/nice/interesting
Example: "I got a new job!" - "That's cool!"
Usage: Positive reaction
8. I feel you ›
Meaning: I understand, I relate to that
Example: "I'm so tired!" - "I feel you."
Usage: Showing empathy
9. Sounds good ›
Meaning: I agree, that works for me
Example: "Let's meet at 5?" - "Sounds good!"
Usage: Agreeing to plans
10. You bet ›
Meaning: Definitely, of course
Example: "Thanks for helping!" - "You bet!"
Usage: Enthusiastic agreement
11. No way! ›
Meaning: I can't believe it! / Absolutely not
Example: "She got promoted!" - "No way!"
Usage: Surprise or strong refusal
12. Fair enough ›
Meaning: That's reasonable, I accept that
Example: "I can't make it tonight." - "Fair enough."
Usage: Accepting someone's point
13. Never mind ›
Meaning: Forget it, it's not important
Example: "What did you say?" - "Never mind."
Usage: Dropping a subject
14. I'm beat ›
Meaning: I'm very tired
Example: "I'm beat - long day at work."
Usage: Expressing exhaustion
15. Take care ›
Meaning: Goodbye (with warmth)
Example: "See you soon - take care!"
Usage: Friendly goodbye
16. Gotcha ›
Meaning: I understand / I got you
Example: "Meet me at the station." - "Gotcha!"
Usage: Acknowledging understanding
17. Totally ›
Meaning: Completely, I totally agree
Example: "This movie is great!" - "Totally!"
Usage: Strong agreement
18. How come? ›
Meaning: Why?
Example: "I can't go." - "How come?"
Usage: Casual way to ask why
19. Hang on ›
Meaning: Wait a moment
Example: "Hang on, I'll be right back."
Usage: Asking someone to wait
20. That sucks ›
Meaning: That's unfortunate/bad
Example: "I failed my test." - "That sucks!"
Usage: Sympathizing with bad news
