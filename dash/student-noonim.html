� Casual vs Formal Conversation Questions
😊 Casual Questions ▼

Questions to ask:

  • "What do you do?"
  • "Where are you from?"
  • "How's work going?"
  • "What company are you with?"
  • "How long have you been there?"

Ways to respond:

  • "I'm a Sales Rep for a tech company."
  • "I'm from Brazil, but I work here now."
  • "Pretty good! We just finished a big project."
  • "Yeah, it's a cool place to work."
  • "About 3 years now. Really enjoying it."
🎩 Formal Questions ▼

Questions to ask:

  • "May I ask what your position is?"
  • "What is your company's primary focus?"
  • "How many years of experience do you have in this field?"
  • "What are your main responsibilities?"
  • "Could you tell me about your organization's structure?"

Professional responses:

  • "I hold the position of Senior Project Manager."
  • "Our company specializes in sustainable energy solutions."
  • "I have been with the organization for five years."
  • "I oversee client relations and project delivery."
  • "We operate with a centralized management structure."

Break down

"Let me break down the quarterly results for you."
Bring up
To mention or introduce a topic
"He brought up the issue of budget allocation in the meeting."
Call off
To cancel or postpone
"They called off the conference due to technical issues."
Come up with
To think of an idea or solution
"Our team came up with an innovative marketing strategy."
Draw up
To prepare or draft a document
"Please draw up a contract for the new partnership."
Follow up
To check on progress or take further action
"I'll follow up with clients about their feedback."
Keep up with
To stay at the same level or not fall behind
"We need to keep up with industry trends."
Push back
To resist or delay something
"The client wants to push back the deadline."
Step up
To increase effort or take responsibility
"We need to step up our marketing efforts."

💬 Language at Work: Present Simple & Possessives

Company Example: Marcegaglia

Company: Marcegaglia (Italian steel pipes company)
Head Office: Near Milan, Italy
CEOs: Antonio Marcegaglia & Emma Marcegaglia (siblings)
Size: Multi-billion euro company with 7,000 employees
Geographic Reach: Customers worldwide - Brazil, China, and many other countries
Type: Family company started by Steno Marcegaglia in 1959

Grammar: Present Simple with "be"

Subject Positive Negative Question
I am / 'm am not ('m not) Am I...?
You/We/They are / 're are not (aren't) Are you/we/they...?
He/She/It is / 's is not (isn't) Is he/she/it...?

Tip - Speaking vs Writing: Use contractions in speaking and emails (I'm, She's, They're). Use full forms in short answers: "Yes, I am." (NOT "Yes, I'm")

Exercise 4: Marcegaglia Interview - Choose Correct Verb

A: Is / Are Marcegaglia a family company?

B: Yes, it is / am. Steno Marcegaglia started the company in 1959, and his children Antonio and Emma is / are the CEOs.

A: Is / Are they from a big family?

B: No, they 's / 're from a small family, but Marcegaglia isn't / 'm not a small company. It 's / 're a multi-billion euro company with 7,000 employees.

A: And is / are all the employees in Italy?

B: They is / are in Italy and in many other countries too, such as Brazil and China.

✓ Answer Key: 1.is 2.is 3.are 4.Are 5.'re 6.isn't 7.'s 8.are 9.are

Subject Possessive Example

Contractions: Speaking vs Writing

⭐ VERY IMPORTANT FOR SPEAKING:

Positive Form Contraction Example
I am I'm "I'm an engineer" (SPEAKING)
You are You're "You're from Brazil" (SPEAKING)
He is He's "He's a manager" (SPEAKING)
She is She's "She's a CEO" (SPEAKING)
It is It's "It's a great company" (SPEAKING)
We are We're "We're from Italy" (SPEAKING)
They are They're "They're engineers" (SPEAKING)

Remember: Contractions are very natural in SPEAKING and emails. Students must use contractions to sound natural!

Negative Forms

Subject Negative (Full) Negative (Contraction)
I am not 'm not
You/We/They are not aren't
He/She/It is not isn't

⚠️ Common Mistake: "I not am from Italy" ❌ → "I'm not from Italy" ✅

Note: "amn't" does NOT exist. We say "I'm not" (NOT "I amn't")

Questions with "To Be"

Structure: Verb + Subject + ?

Are you a manager? (Question: Are + you)
Is she from Brazil? (Question: Is + she)
Is it a family company? (Question: Is + it)
Are they engineers? (Question: Are + they)

Short Answers:

Question Positive Answer Negative Answer
Are you...? Yes, I am No, I'm not
Is she...? Yes, she is No, she isn't
Are they...? Yes, they are No, they aren't

⚠️ CRITICAL RULE: Short answers do NOT use contractions! "Yes, I am" ✅ (NOT "Yes, I'm") ❌

Articles with Jobs

Rule: Use "a" or "an" before a single job

Remember: "an" before vowel SOUNDS (not just vowel letters!)

✓ "I am a manager" (m = consonant sound)
✓ "She is an engineer" (e = vowel sound)
✓ "He is an accountant" (a = vowel sound)
✓ "I work as an assistant" (a = vowel sound)
❌ "She is engineer" (missing article)
❌ "I am a accountant" (wrong article for vowel sound)

Sentence Pattern: Introduction Template

Use this pattern to introduce yourself:

Name: "My name is [NAME]"
Country: "I am from [COUNTRY]"
Nationality: "I am [NATIONALITY]"
Job: "I am a/an [JOB]"

Example:

"My name is Sofia Aguilera. I am from Mexico. I am Mexican. I am a managing director."

💼 Business Communication: Saying Hello & Goodbye

Introduction & First Meetings

• "Hello. My name is Alek Gorski."
• "I'm Eva, Maria's assistant."
• "Pleased to meet you."
• "Nice to meet you."
• "How do you do?" (Very formal - reply with: "How do you do?")

Introducing Someone Else

• "This is my assistant, Elzbieta Wozniak."
• "Do you know Alek?" → Response: "No, how do you do?"

Greeting Someone You Know

• "It's good to see you again." → Response: "And you."
• "How are you?" → Response: "I'm fine, thanks." or "Not bad."

Saying Goodbye

• "Nice meeting you." / "Nice meeting you, too."
• "See you soon."
• "Have a good journey."
• "Goodbye." / "Bye."

📝 Homework & Self-Study Tasks

Task 1: Write a Company Profile (Like Marcegaglia)

Write 5-8 sentences about a company (real or imaginary). Include:

• Company name, location, products/services
• CEO or leader name and nationality
• Company size and global reach
• Type of company (family, multinational, etc.)

Task 2: Personal Introduction Recording (1-2 minutes)

Record yourself saying:

• "Hello. My name is _____. I'm from [COUNTRY]. I'm [NATIONALITY]. I'm a/an [JOB]. My company is [COMPANY NAME]. It's a [NATIONALITY] company."
• Speak slowly and clearly
• Try to sound professional

Task 3: Spelling Recording

Record yourself spelling clearly:

• Your full name: letter by letter
• Your company name: letter by letter
• Your job title: word by word

Task 4: Write a Conversation (10-20 lines)

Create a dialogue between two people meeting for the first time. Include:

• Greetings and introductions
• Information about jobs and nationalities
• Goodbye expressions
• Use "be" verbs and possessives correctly

Task 5: The Introductions Game

Play the board game activity with a partner or study partner. Practice:

• Asking questions about names, jobs, nationalities
• Introducing yourself professionally
• Spelling names and companies
• Saying goodbye politely
