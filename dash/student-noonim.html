� Casual vs Formal Conversation Questions
😊 Casual Questions ▼

Questions to ask:

  • "What do you do?"
  • "Where are you from?"
  • "How's work going?"
  • "What company are you with?"
  • "How long have you been there?"

Ways to respond:

  • "I'm a Sales Rep for a tech company."
  • "I'm from Brazil, but I work here now."
  • "Pretty good! We just finished a big project."
  • "Yeah, it's a cool place to work."
  • "About 3 years now. Really enjoying it."
🎩 Formal Questions ▼

Questions to ask:

  • "May I ask what your position is?"
  • "What is your company's primary focus?"
  • "How many years of experience do you have in this field?"
  • "What are your main responsibilities?"
  • "Could you tell me about your organization's structure?"

Professional responses:

  • "I hold the position of Senior Project Manager."
  • "Our company specializes in sustainable energy solutions."
  • "I have been with the organization for five years."
  • "I oversee client relations and project delivery."
  • "We operate with a centralized management structure."

🟢 Easy Phrasal Verbs (10)

1. Wake up ›
Definition: To stop sleeping
Example: "I wake up at 7am every day."
Business: "We need to wake up early for the meeting."
2. Turn on / Turn off ›
Definition: To start / stop a machine or device
Example: "Turn on the computer please."
Business: "Can you turn off the projector?"
3. Look at ›
Definition: To see or view something
Example: "Look at this photo!"
Business: "Let's look at the sales report."
4. Pick up ›
Definition: To lift something; to collect someone
Example: "Can you pick up the phone?"
Business: "I'll pick up the client from the airport."
5. Come in ›
Definition: To enter a room or building
Example: "Please come in and sit down."
Business: "Come in to my office, please."
6. Sit down ›
Definition: To take a seat
Example: "Please sit down."
Business: "Let's sit down and discuss this."
7. Write down ›
Definition: To record information on paper
Example: "Write down your phone number."
Business: "Please write down the meeting notes."
8. Send out ›
Definition: To distribute or mail something
Example: "I'll send out the email today."
Business: "We need to send out the invitations."
9. Fill in ›
Definition: To complete a form or document
Example: "Please fill in this form."
Business: "Fill in your name and email address."
10. Find out ›
Definition: To discover or learn information
Example: "I need to find out his email."
Business: "Let me find out who is responsible."

💬 Language at Work: Present Simple & Possessives

Company Example: Marcegaglia

Company: Marcegaglia (Italian steel pipes company)
Head Office: Near Milan, Italy
CEOs: Antonio Marcegaglia & Emma Marcegaglia (siblings)
Size: Multi-billion euro company with 7,000 employees
Geographic Reach: Customers worldwide - Brazil, China, and many other countries
Type: Family company started by Steno Marcegaglia in 1959

Grammar: Present Simple with "be"

Subject Positive Negative Question
I am / 'm am not ('m not) Am I...?
You/We/They are / 're are not (aren't) Are you/we/they...?
He/She/It is / 's is not (isn't) Is he/she/it...?

Tip - Speaking vs Writing: Use contractions in speaking and emails (I'm, She's, They're). Use full forms in short answers: "Yes, I am." (NOT "Yes, I'm")

Contractions: Speaking vs Writing

⭐ VERY IMPORTANT FOR SPEAKING:

Positive Form Contraction Example
I am I'm "I'm an engineer" (SPEAKING)
You are You're "You're from Brazil" (SPEAKING)
He is He's "He's a manager" (SPEAKING)
She is She's "She's a CEO" (SPEAKING)
It is It's "It's a great company" (SPEAKING)
We are We're "We're from Italy" (SPEAKING)
They are They're "They're engineers" (SPEAKING)

Remember: Contractions are very natural in SPEAKING and emails. Students must use contractions to sound natural!

Negative Forms

Subject Negative (Full) Negative (Contraction)
I am not 'm not
You/We/They are not aren't
He/She/It is not isn't

⚠️ Common Mistake: "I not am from Italy" ❌ → "I'm not from Italy" ✅

Note: "amn't" does NOT exist. We say "I'm not" (NOT "I amn't")

Questions with "To Be"

Structure: Verb + Subject + ?

Are you a manager? (Question: Are + you)
Is she from Brazil? (Question: Is + she)
Is it a family company? (Question: Is + it)
Are they engineers? (Question: Are + they)

Short Answers:

Question Positive Answer Negative Answer
Are you...? Yes, I am No, I'm not
Is she...? Yes, she is No, she isn't
Are they...? Yes, they are No, they aren't

⚠️ CRITICAL RULE: Short answers do NOT use contractions! "Yes, I am" ✅ (NOT "Yes, I'm") ❌

Articles with Jobs

Rule: Use "a" or "an" before a single job

Remember: "an" before vowel SOUNDS (not just vowel letters!)

✓ "I am a manager" (m = consonant sound)
✓ "She is an engineer" (e = vowel sound)
✓ "He is an accountant" (a = vowel sound)
✓ "I work as an assistant" (a = vowel sound)
❌ "She is engineer" (missing article)
❌ "I am a accountant" (wrong article for vowel sound)

💼 Business Communication: Saying Hello & Goodbye

Introduction & First Meetings

• "Hello. My name is Alek Gorski."
• "I'm Eva, Maria's assistant."
• "Pleased to meet you."
• "Nice to meet you."

• "This is my assistant, Elzbieta Wozniak."

• "It's good to see you again." → Response: "And you."

Saying Goodbye

• "Nice meeting you." / "Nice meeting you, too."
• "See you soon."
• "Have a good journey."
• "Goodbye." / "Bye."

💬 Language at Work: Present Simple

Why do we use Present Simple?

We use the present simple for general facts and regular activities about companies.

✓ "Toyota produces cars." (general fact - always true)
✓ "We export to 100 countries." (regular activity)
✓ "The company employs 6,000 people." (general fact)

Present Simple Rules

Subject Positive Negative Question
I / You / We / They produce don't produce Do you produce...?
He / She / It produces doesn't produce Does it produce...?

⭐ KEY RULE: Add -S or -ES for he/she/it

This is very common mistake! Don't forget the -S!

✅ "The company produces cars." (it = company)
✅ "Sony exports to Europe." (it = Sony)
✅ "She works in finance."
❌ "The company produce cars." (WRONG - missing -s)

Spelling Rules for -S/-ES:

• Most verbs: add -s → produce → produces
• Verbs ending in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: add -es → do → does
• Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to -ies → employ → employs (NOT -ies because 'oy')

Special Verb: HAVE → HAS

⚠️ "have" is irregular!

I/You/We/They have → "We have 5 offices."
He/She/It has → "The company has 6,000 employees."
❌ "It haves" → NEVER! Always "It has"

Making Questions

Structure: DO/DOES + subject + verb (base form)?

Question Yes Answer No Answer
Do you export products? Yes, we do. No, we don't.
Does the company import films? Yes, it does. No, it doesn't.
Does CJ provide financial services? Yes, it does. No, it doesn't.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: In questions with DOES, the main verb has NO -s!

✅ "Does Sony produce TVs?" (NOT "Does Sony produces")
✅ "Does she work in finance?" (NOT "Does she works")

Making Negatives

Structure: subject + DON'T/DOESN'T + verb (base form)

We don't import medicines. (We = plural)
The company doesn't provide financial services. (company = it)
She doesn't work in marketing.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: After DOESN'T, the verb has NO -s!

✅ "The company doesn't produce cars." (NOT "doesn't produces")

Short Answers - Very Important for Speaking!

⭐ In conversation, we use SHORT answers!

A: "Do you export products?" → B: "Yes, we do." (NOT "Yes, we export.")
A: "Does Toyota produce software?" → B: "No, it doesn't." (NOT "No, it doesn't produce.")
A: "Do you work in finance?" → B: "No, I don't. I work in marketing."

💬 Language at Work: Past Simple – Irregular Verbs

Irregular Verbs – These do NOT use -ed!

⭐ These verbs change completely in the past tense. You must memorize them!

Base (Present) Past Simple Business Example
go went "I went to a conference in Bangkok."
have had "We had a meeting yesterday."
meet met "I met some great contacts at the event."
speak spoke "She spoke to the CEO last week."
take took "He took a business trip to Singapore."
see saw "I saw your post on LinkedIn."
come came "The client came to our office."
get got "I got a lot of new contacts."
give gave "She gave a great presentation."
send sent "I sent him a connection request."
write wrote "She wrote a post about the event."
make made "We made new connections at the fair."

⭐ Key Rule: For negatives and questions, use didn't + base verb — NOT the past form!
✅ "I didn't go to the conference." (NOT "didn't went")
✅ "Did you meet anyone useful?" (NOT "Did you met")

Negatives and Questions – Irregular Verbs

Form Structure Example
Positive subject + past verb "I went to the trade fair."
Negative didn't + base verb "I didn't go to the trade fair."
Question Did + subject + base verb? "Did you go to the trade fair?"
Short answer Yes, I did. / No, I didn't. "Did you meet anyone?" "Yes, I did."

❌ "Did you went to the event?" → Use BASE VERB after Did!
✅ "Did you go to the event?"

🔊 Pronunciation: Word Stress in Two-Syllable Words

Stress Patterns – First or Second Syllable?

In English, two-syllable nouns and verbs often have different stress patterns. Getting stress right makes you clearer and more professional when speaking.

Stress on 1st syllable (NOUN) Stress on 2nd syllable (VERB)
PRE-sent (a gift) pre-SENT (to give or show)
RE-cord (a CD) re-CORD (to film/record)
CON-tent (material) con-TENT (satisfied)
UP-date (latest news) up-DATE (to refresh)
NET-work (contacts) net-WORK (to make connections)

📌 Tip: "I received a PRE-sent from my client." (noun) vs. "I will pre-SENT the results to the team." (verb)

🤝 Business Skills: Small Talk & Making General Conversation

What is Small Talk?

Small talk is light, friendly conversation used to build relationships before business discussions. In networking, small talk is very important!

• Talk about travel / trips – "Did you have a good journey?"
• Talk about the event – "Is this your first time here?"
• Talk about work – "What do you do? How's business?"
• Talk about the city / place – "Have you been to Bangkok before?"
• Avoid: personal topics (salary, age, politics, religion)

Useful Phrases – Starting a Conversation

• "Hi, I don't think we've met. I'm [name]."
• "Are you enjoying the conference?"
• "What did you think of the presentation?"
• "Which company are you with?"
• "Did you come far to get here?"
• "Is this your first time at this event?"

Useful Phrases – Keeping the Conversation Going

• "That's interesting! Tell me more about that."
• "Really? How long have you been there?"
• "And what are you working on at the moment?"
• "What did you think of [topic/trend]?"
• "Have you tried the [food/venue] here?"

Useful Phrases – Ending a Conversation Politely

• "It was great meeting you. Here's my card."
• "I'll connect with you on LinkedIn."
• "I should go and find some other contacts, but let's stay in touch."
• "I hope to speak again soon. Enjoy the rest of the event!"
