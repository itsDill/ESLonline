G1 · Present Simple of be — Units 1 & 2 ▼
Subject Positive Negative Question
I I am (I'm) I am not (I'm not) Am I…?
You / We / They You are (You're) You aren't Are you…?
He / She / It He is (He's) He isn't Is he…?
📘 Use: jobs, nationalities, descriptions. "I'm a manager." / "She's from Thailand."
✏️ Practice — Complete with am / is / are
  1. She _____ a project manager at a tech company.
  2. We _____ from the Bangkok office.
  3. _____ you free on Friday?
  4. He _____ not in the meeting right now.
  5. They _____ both engineers.
1. is  ·  2. are  ·  3. Are  ·  4. is  ·  5. are

Form Structure Example

🔊 Pronunciation Review: Word Endings

Focus practice from your book language: -s / -es and -d / -ed endings, with business examples and hints.

1) Words ending in -s / -es

/s/ after voiceless sounds (p, t, k, f): works, exports, likes, helps
/z/ after vowels / voiced sounds: has, needs, lives, calls, plays
/ɪz/ after s, sh, ch, x, z, j sounds: watches, finishes, changes, prices

💡 Hint: Touch your throat. Vibration usually means /z/; no vibration usually means /s/.

2) Words ending in -d / -ed

/t/ after voiceless sounds: worked, watched, asked, stopped
/d/ after vowels / voiced sounds: called, opened, emailed, arrived
/ɪd/ only after t or d: started, needed, visited, decided

💡 Hint: If the base verb ends in t or d, add an extra syllable: /ɪd/.

3) Book-style Examples (Units 2–8 language)

She works in HR. → /s/
Our company exports cars. → /s/
He has a meeting at 10. → /z/
The team watches market trends. → /ɪz/
We worked late yesterday. → /t/
She called the client. → /d/
They started at 9:00. → /ɪd/

4) Quick Practice (tap to reveal)

1. She works in sales. Final -s sound? /s/
2. He has two interviews today. Final -s sound? /z/
3. We watched the presentation. -ed sound? /t/
4. I emailed the report. -ed sound? /d/
5. They visited our office. -ed sound? /ɪd/

📋 Unit 1: Jobs & Introductions ▼
🎯 Key Topics: Countries, nationalities, jobs, workplace introductions
Discussion Questions:
  • What is your job title? What do you do exactly?
  • How do you introduce yourself in a professional setting?
  • What company do you work for? What does your company do?
  • Where is your company's head office located?
  • What nationalities are most common in your workplace?

  • "I work for..." vs "I work at..." — When to use each
  • Describing your job responsibilities with present simple
  • Making small talk about where you're from
📦 Unit 2: Products & Services ▼
🎯 Key Topics: Company types, phone calls, numbers, products/services
Discussion Questions:
  • What products or services does your company offer?
  • How do you answer the phone professionally?
  • Can you describe your company's main product in detail?
  • What type of company is it? (multinational, startup, etc.)
  • How do you spell your email address over the phone?
💡 Talking Points:
  • Phone etiquette: "May I speak to...?" vs "Can I talk to...?"
  • Saying numbers, prices, and phone numbers clearly
  • Describing features and benefits of products
📍 Unit 3: Location ▼
🎯 Key Topics: Workplace locations, addresses, prepositions of place, ordering
Discussion Questions:
  • Where is your office located? Can you describe the area?
  • How do you describe directions to your workplace?
  • What facilities are near your office? (bank, restaurant, etc.)
  • How do you order food or drinks in English?
  • What's the difference between "on the corner" and "at the corner"?
💡 Talking Points:
  • Prepositions: in, on, at, next to, opposite, between
  • Ordering: "I'd like..." vs "Can I have...?"
  • Using "there is" and "there are" for locations
💻 Unit 4: Technology ▼
🎯 Key Topics: Technology vocabulary, work activities, asking for help
Discussion Questions:
  • What technology do you use every day at work?
  • How do you ask for help with technical problems?
  • What software or apps are essential for your job?
  • How do you explain a technical issue to IT support?
  • What are your typical daily work activities?
💡 Talking Points:
  • "Could you help me with...?" — Polite requests
  • Present continuous for current activities
  • Tech vocabulary: log in, restart, update, download, upload
📧 Unit 5: Communication ▼
🎯 Key Topics: Documents, past events, emails, problem solving
Discussion Questions:
  • What types of documents do you work with?
  • How do you describe what happened at a recent meeting?
  • What was your biggest challenge last week at work?
  • How do you handle communication problems?
  • What's the difference between formal and informal emails?
💡 Talking Points:
  • Past simple tense: "I sent...", "We discussed..."
  • Problem-solving phrases: "The issue was...", "We solved it by..."
  • Email vocabulary: attachment, forward, CC, reply all
🤝 Unit 6: Networking ▼
🎯 Key Topics: Social media, business trips, conversation skills
Discussion Questions:
  • How do you use social media for business?

  • How do you start a conversation at a networking event?

  • How do you stay connected with business contacts?
💡 Talking Points:
  • Small talk starters: "How was your flight?", "Is this your first time here?"
  • LinkedIn vocabulary: connect, post, share, comment
  • Describing trips: past simple for completed trips
🏢 Unit 7: Departments ▼
🎯 Key Topics: Company departments, giving directions, phone messages
Discussion Questions:
  • What departments does your company have?
  • Which department do you work with most often?
  • How do you give directions inside an office building?
  • How do you take a phone message for someone?
  • What does the HR / Finance / Marketing department do?
💡 Talking Points:
  • Department names: HR, Finance, Marketing, Sales, IT, R&D
  • Directions: "Go straight", "Turn left", "Take the elevator to..."
  • Phone messages: "Can I take a message?", "I'll ask them to call you back"

💬 Language at Work: Present Continuous

Present Continuous – Talking About Now

Use present continuous for actions happening right now or around now.

Subject am/is/are verb + ing Example
I am working "I am working on a report."
He/She/It is meeting "She is meeting a client."
We/You/They are preparing "They are preparing for the conference."

⭐ Contractions: I'm working, He's meeting, They're preparing

Present Continuous – Questions & Negatives

Form Structure Example
Positive subject + am/is/are + verb-ing "He is interviewing candidates."
Negative subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing "He isn't interviewing today."
Question Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing? "Are you looking for a new job?"
Short answer Yes, I am. / No, I'm not. "Is she working today?" "Yes, she is."

Practice: Present Continuous

1. I ____ (write) my CV at the moment. → am writing
2. She ____ (prepare) for the interview. → is preparing
