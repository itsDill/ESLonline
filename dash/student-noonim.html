💼 Business Phrasal Verbs
Break down
To analyze or explain in detail
"Let me break down the quarterly results for you."
Bring up
To mention or introduce a topic
"He brought up the issue of budget allocation in the meeting."
Call off
To cancel or postpone
"They called off the conference due to technical issues."
Come up with
To think of an idea or solution
"Our team came up with an innovative marketing strategy."
Draw up
To prepare or draft a document
"Please draw up a contract for the new partnership."
Follow up
To check on progress or take further action
"I'll follow up with clients about their feedback."
Keep up with
To stay at the same level or not fall behind
"We need to keep up with industry trends."
Push back
To resist or delay something
"The client wants to push back the deadline."
Step up
To increase effort or take responsibility
"We need to step up our marketing efforts."
💼 Business Idioms
Hit the ground running
To start immediately and with great energy
"His team hit the ground running on the new project."
Knock it out of the park
To do something extremely well
"She knocked it out of the park with her presentation!"
Level the playing field
To create fair and equal conditions
"New regulations will level the playing field for all competitors."
Make a long story short
To summarize the main point briefly
"Make a long story short, we lost the contract."
Run a tight ship
To manage efficiently and strictly
"Our manager runs a tight ship with clear procedures."
Seal the deal
To finalize or close an agreement
"They shook hands to seal the deal."
Think outside the box
To be creative and find unconventional solutions
"We need to think outside the box to compete."
Take off
To start being successful; to grow rapidly
"The new product line really took off in Q2."
Wrap up
To finish or conclude something
"Let's wrap up today's meeting by 5 PM."

📖 Weekly Lesson Plan

Week 1: Company Culture & Introductions

▼
🎯 Learning Objectives
  • Understand company culture and workplace communication
  • Learn to introduce yourself professionally in business
  • Master key business phrasal verbs: Break down, Bring up
  • Practice formal email greetings and closings
  • Develop skills for first meetings and networking
💬 Grammar Focus: Present Tenses & Reported Speech

Present Simple (routines and facts)

Our company focuses on innovation and customer satisfaction.
We work from 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM, Monday to Friday.

Present Perfect (experience and updates)

I've worked in the tech industry for five years.
We've recently expanded to three new markets.
🎓 Activities & Practice

1. Professional Introduction Role Play

Record yourself introducing your background, current role, and company. Focus on: "Hi, I'm [NAME]. I work as a [ROLE] at [COMPANY]. I'm responsible for [MAIN DUTIES]. In my free time, I enjoy [INTEREST]."

2. Formal Email Writing

Write an introductory email to a business contact. Include: greeting, purpose, relevant background, and professional closing. Example: "To whom it may concern / Dear [Name], I am writing to introduce myself..."

3. Company Culture Discussion

Prepare a 2-minute presentation about your company (real or hypothetical): company values, mission, team size, key achievements, and workplace culture.

4. Listening Exercise

Listen to the Oxford Business Result Unit 1 audio interviews (Track 1-3). Note down: job titles, responsibilities, company names, and interesting facts about speakers.

📚 Week 1 Vocabulary Flashcards

Bring up

Key account

Responsibilities
Duties and tasks that are assigned to someone in a job
Liaise with
Communicate and maintain contact between different groups or departments
