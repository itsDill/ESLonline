🚀 Starting Point▶
Discuss these questions

  3. Where is your head office?
🏢 Working with Words: Location and Workplace▶
1) Key workplaces
Factory - We make all our products here.
Research and development (R&D) centre - We design new products here.
Sales office - Sales reps visit customers, then return here.
Head office - Directors and top management work here.
Distribution centre - Products arrive here, then go to customers.
2) Reading: LEGO Group
LEGO produces play materials for children and sells products in more than 140 countries. It is a Danish company and the name comes from leg godt (play well). It operates on six continents and has about 14,000 employees. There are also LEGOLAND parks in Asia, Europe and North America.
Tip: about / around = approximately. Example: about 850 employees, around 100 countries.

🧠 Language at Work: There is/are + Some/Any▶
1) Why Singapore is good for business
Key ideas from the text: strategic location in South-East Asia, flights to about 300 cities, strong harbour, low business taxes, easy visas, many hotels and exhibition centres, excellent schools/hospitals, and low crime.
2) Grammar table
Positive Negative Question Short answer
Singular noun There is an airport. There isn't an airport. Is there an airport? Yes, there is. / No, there isn't.
Plural noun There are two airports. There aren't two airports. Are there two airports? Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.
We use some and any with plural nouns. Use some in positive sentences. Use any in negative sentences and questions.
3) Gap fill practice (from listening task)

A: Dubai is a great location for a conference. The weather is always good.
B: What about the airport? ____ lots of international flights?
A: Yes, ____. And ____ a problem with transport from the airport...
B: But ____ a good place for a conference?
A: Yes, ____. It's the Dubai International Exhibition and Convention complex.

📞 Business Communication: Ordering by Phone▶
Key expressions
Function Useful language
Asking to do something Can I order ... ?
Asking for information Can you tell me ... ? / Can I have ... ? / Can you confirm ... ?
Asking for repetition Can you repeat that? / Can you say that more slowly, please?
Responding yes Yes, of course. / Sure.
Responding no I'm sorry, but ...
Confirming Got it. / Yes, that's right.
Role-play from the book
