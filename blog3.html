Phrasal Verbs That Pack a Punch

Essential Daily Phrasal Verbs MUST KNOW

These aren't just words - they're the building blocks of natural English conversation! Click "Reveal" to see the meaning and examples.

Give Up
To stop trying or quit something
"Don't give up on your dreams!" | "I gave up smoking last year."
Run Into
To meet someone by chance or encounter unexpectedly
"I ran into my old teacher at the store." | "We ran into some problems with the project."
Put Off
To postpone or delay something
"I can't put off studying any longer!" | "The meeting was put off until next week."
Look Up
To search for information or visit someone
"I need to look up that word." | "Look me up when you're in town!"
Break Down
To stop working OR to explain in detail OR to become upset
"My car broke down." | "Let me break down the costs." | "She broke down in tears."
Show Off
To display something proudly or act in a boastful way
"He loves to show off his new car." | "Stop showing off and help us!"
Turn Up
To arrive or appear, or to increase volume/intensity
"She didn't turn up to the meeting." | "Turn up the music!"
Get Along
To have a good relationship with someone
"We get along really well." | "How are you getting along with your new roommate?"

Idioms That Make You Sound Like a Local

Mind-Blowing English Idioms ADVANCED

These colorful expressions will make your English pop with personality! Click to reveal meanings.

"It's raining cats and dogs!" 🌧️
It's raining very heavily
"We can't go out now - it's raining cats and dogs!"
"Break a leg!" 🎭
Good luck! (especially for performances)
"Break a leg in your job interview!" | "Break a leg tonight - you'll be amazing!"
"Spill the tea!" ☕
Tell me the gossip or news (modern slang)
"Come on, spill the tea about what happened!" | "She finally spilled the tea about her breakup."
"Hit the hay" 💤
Go to sleep
"I'm exhausted, time to hit the hay!" | "We should hit the hay early tonight."
"Costs an arm and a leg" 💸
Very expensive
"That designer bag costs an arm and a leg!" | "Private school costs an arm and a leg these days."
"Bite the bullet" 😤
Do something difficult but necessary
"I need to bite the bullet and call my boss." | "Just bite the bullet and pay the fine."
"Over the moon" 🌙
Extremely happy and excited
"I'm over the moon about my promotion!" | "She was over the moon when she got accepted."
"Piece of cake" 🍰
Something very easy to do
"The exam was a piece of cake!" | "Don't worry, this recipe is a piece of cake."

The Art of Exaggeration

Turn Up the Drama! FUN

Native speakers LOVE to exaggerate! It makes conversations more engaging and expressive. Master these patterns to sound more natural!

Normal vs. DRAMATIC! 🎭
Regular: "I'm tired."
Regular: "It's cold."
Regular: "I'm hungry."
Regular: "That's funny."
Regular: "I'm busy."
Regular: "It's expensive."
DRAMATIC: "I'm absolutely exhausted!"
DRAMATIC: "It's freezing cold!"
DRAMATIC: "I'm starving to death!"
DRAMATIC: "That's hilarious!"
DRAMATIC: "I'm swamped with work!"
DRAMATIC: "It costs a fortune!"
