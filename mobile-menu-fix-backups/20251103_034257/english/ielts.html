IELTS Overview

The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is one of the world's most popular English language proficiency tests. It's designed to assess the language ability of candidates who need to study or work where English is used as the language of communication.

IELTS is recognized by more than 10,000 organizations worldwide, including schools, universities, employers, immigration authorities and professional bodies. It's the world's proven English test.

Time Management Mastery

Learn to allocate time effectively across all IELTS sections. Practice with timed exercises to build speed and accuracy under pressure.

Time Allocation:

Listening Section Pitfalls

Success Strategy:

IELTS FAQs

Get answers to the most frequently asked questions about IELTS

Test Information

How long are IELTS scores valid?

IELTS scores are valid for 2 years from the test date. After this period, you'll need to retake the test if required by your institution or organization.

What's the difference between Academic and General Training?

Academic IELTS is for university study, while General Training is for work or immigration. They differ mainly in Reading and Writing tasks, with Academic being more academic-focused.

Preparation Tips

How long should I prepare for IELTS?

Preparation time varies by current level and target score. Generally, 2-4 months of consistent daily practice (1-2 hours) is recommended for most students.

Can I retake IELTS if I'm not happy with my score?

Yes, there's no limit on retakes, but you must wait for your results before booking another test. Allow at least 2-3 weeks between tests for additional preparation.
