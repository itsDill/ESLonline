Subject-Verb Agreement Mastery

Make Your Subjects & Verbs Best Friends ESSENTIAL

Think of subject-verb agreement like a dance partnership - they need to move in perfect harmony!

Common Mistake Alert! üö®
‚ùå Wrong:
"The group of students are excited."
"Either John or the teachers is coming."
"There's many reasons why..."
‚úÖ Right:
"The group of students IS excited."
"Either John or the teachers ARE coming."
"There ARE many reasons why..."
Singular subjects = Singular verbs
One person/thing doing one action
"She walks" / "The cat sleeps"
Plural subjects = Plural verbs
Multiple people/things doing actions
"They walk" / "The cats sleep"
Tricky Groups
Group words are usually singular
"The team IS ready" / "The class WAS quiet"

Comma Rules That Actually Make Sense

Comma Superpowers Unlocked! MUST-KNOW

Commas are like traffic signals for your sentences - they tell readers when to pause and when to keep going!

Oxford Comma Hero
Use before "and" in lists of 3+
"Pizza, burgers, and tacos are amazing!"
Compound Sentence Connector
Before conjunctions joining complete thoughts
"I love movies, but I prefer books."
Intro Element Separator
After introductory words/phrases
"After the game, we got pizza."
Nonessential Info Wrapper
Around extra information
"My teacher, who is hilarious, loves puns."

Pronoun Power-Ups

Pronouns That Pack a Punch ESSENTIAL

Pronouns are the ultimate multitaskers - they replace nouns and keep your writing smooth and natural!

Subject vs Object
I/he/she/we/they vs me/him/her/us/them
"Between you and ME" (not "you and I")
Who vs Whom
Who = subject, Whom = object
"WHO is coming?" / "To WHOM are you talking?"
Pronoun Agreement
Match the number and gender
"Everyone should bring THEIR book" (singular they)
