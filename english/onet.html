About O-NET

What is O-NET?

The Ordinary National Educational Test (O-NET) is a standardized test administered by the National Institute of Educational Testing Service (NIETS) in Thailand. It assesses students' knowledge and competency across core subjects.

Who Takes It?

Students in Mathayom 3 (Grade 9) and Mathayom 6 (Grade 12) take the O-NET test. It's used for school admissions, university placement, and measuring educational quality nationwide.

Scoring System

Scores range from 0-100 for each subject. The test uses criterion-referenced scoring to measure students against national educational standards.

Expert Study Tips

Create a Study Schedule

Dedicate specific time slots for each subject. Consistency is key to retaining information and building strong foundations.

Active Reading

Don't just read - engage with the material. Take notes, ask questions, and summarize key points in your own words.

Study Groups

Join or form study groups with classmates. Teaching others reinforces your own understanding.

Regular Review

Review material regularly using spaced repetition. This helps transfer knowledge to long-term memory.

Practice Problems

Work through as many practice problems as possible. This builds confidence and reveals areas needing improvement.

Healthy Habits

Get adequate sleep, eat well, and exercise regularly. A healthy body supports a sharp mind.
