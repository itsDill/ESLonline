Essential Vocabulary

Master these ~2,000 words for Grade 3

Abstract Concepts

  • experience - something that happens to you
    → Example: "I have had many interesting experiences while traveling."
  • opportunity - a chance to do something
    → Example: "This job is a great opportunity for me."
  • improve - to make or become better
    → Example: "My English has improved a lot this year."
  • achieve - to successfully complete something
    → Example: "She achieved her goal of passing the exam."
  • suggest - to propose an idea
    → Example: "I suggest that we study together."

Common Expressions

  • look forward to - to be excited about something
    → Example: "I'm looking forward to seeing you."
  • take care of - to be responsible for
    → Example: "She takes care of her younger brother."
  • find out - to discover information
    → Example: "I need to find out when the test is."
  • give up - to stop trying
    → Example: "Don't give up! Keep studying!"
  • used to - past habit that is no longer true
    → Example: "I used to play piano, but I stopped."

Travel & Culture

  • foreign - from another country
    → Example: "I want to visit foreign countries."
  • culture - customs and traditions of a group
    → Example: "Japanese culture is very interesting."
  • exchange - to trade or swap
    → Example: "Our school has an exchange program."
  • adventure - an exciting experience
    → Example: "Traveling alone was a great adventure."
  • custom - a traditional way of doing something
    → Example: "It's a custom to take off shoes indoors."

Grammar Focus

Key grammar points for Grade 3

Present Perfect

Use for actions that started in the past and continue now, or have relevance to now

Examples:

  • ✓ I have studied English for five years. (still studying)
  • ✓ She has visited Tokyo three times. (life experience)
  • ✓ We haven't finished our homework yet. (negative)
  • ✓ Have you ever eaten sushi? (question)

Conditionals (If sentences)

First conditional for real future possibilities

Examples:

  • ✓ If it rains, I will stay home. (if + present, will + verb)
  • ✓ If you study hard, you will pass the test.
  • ✓ She won't come if she is busy.
  • ✓ What will you do if you miss the train?

Modal Verbs (should, must, might)

Express possibility, necessity, and advice

Examples:

  • ✓ You should study more. (advice)
  • ✓ We must finish this today. (necessity)
  • ✓ It might rain tomorrow. (possibility)
  • ✓ You mustn't use your phone in class. (prohibition)

Practice Exercises

Test your Grade 3 knowledge

1. I _____ to Australia, so I want to visit someday.

Answer: (c) have never been

"Want to visit someday" shows you've never been there. Use present perfect with "never" for life experiences.

2. If it _____ sunny tomorrow, we will go to the beach.

Answer: (b) is

First conditional: If + present simple, will + verb. Don't use "will" in the if-clause.

3. You _____ try the local food when you travel. It's part of the experience!

Answer: (b) should

"Should" gives advice. "Must" is too strong (necessity), and "might" expresses possibility.

Sample Writing Response

Grade 3 writing task example (25-35 words)

Question: What do you like to do in your free time?

In my free time, I enjoy reading books and playing the guitar. I also like watching movies with my friends on weekends. These activities help me relax after studying.

(32 words)

Writing Tips:

  • ✓ Answer the question directly in the first sentence
  • ✓ Give 2-3 specific examples
  • ✓ Use connecting words: "and", "also", "because"
  • ✓ End with a reason or feeling
  • ✓ Count your words carefully (25-35 range)
