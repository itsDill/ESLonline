What Are Prefixes & Suffixes?

Prefixes and suffixes are letter combinations added to root words to change their meaning or grammatical function. They follow predictable patterns, allowing you to decode unfamiliar words and expand your vocabulary exponentially.

Quick Definitions

Prefix

Added at the BEGINNING of a word to modify its meaning

Example: un- + happy = unhappy

Suffix

Added at the END of a word to change its function or meaning

Example: happy + -ness = happiness

Negation Prefixes

Beginner

These prefixes add negative meaning to words

un-
Not; opposite of
happy → unhappy
clear → unclear
lucky → unlucky
certain → uncertain
in-, im-, il-, ir-
Not; opposite of (before certain consonants)
correct → incorrect
possible → impossible
legal → illegal
responsible → irresponsible
dis-
Not; opposite of; remove
agree → disagree
honest → dishonest
connect → disconnect
like → dislike
mis-
Wrong; badly
understand → misunderstand
interpret → misinterpret
behavior → misbehavior
spell → misspell
non-
Not; without
fiction → nonfiction
smoking → nonsmoking
toxic → nontoxic
stop → nonstop
a-, an-
Not; without
moral → amoral
typical → atypical
anonymous (an- + onym)
atheist (an- + theist)
Key Pattern
Most negation prefixes express "not" or "opposite of." Choose the one that sounds correct in your language - it often depends on the following letter!
