English has 12 main verb tenses that help us express when actions happen. Mastering these tenses is essential for clear communication and natural English fluency.

üîµ Present Tenses (1-4)

Actions happening now, regularly, or with present relevance

1. Present Simple

Beginner Habits
ü§î Can you guess the formula for Present Simple? Hint: "I work every day"
Subject + Base Verb (+ s/es for 3rd person singular)
Examples:
I work every day. (habit)
She studies English at university. (routine)
The sun rises in the east. (fact)
Water boils at 100¬∞C. (scientific fact)
When to use: Habits, routines, general facts, permanent situations, scheduled events.
üí° Memory tip: Think "simple" = basic facts and routines that don't change often!

2. Present Continuous

Beginner Now
ü§î What's the pattern for Present Continuous? Hint: "I am working right now"
Subject + am/is/are + Verb-ing
Examples:
I am working right now. (happening now)
She is studying for her exam this week. (temporary)
They are living with friends temporarily. (temporary)
We are meeting tomorrow at 3 PM. (future plan)
When to use: Actions happening now, temporary situations, future arrangements, trends.
üí° Memory tip: "Continuous" = ongoing action, like a movie that's still playing!

3. Present Perfect

Intermediate Experience
ü§î How do we form Present Perfect? Hint: "I have worked here for 5 years"
Subject + have/has + Past Participle (3rd form)
Examples:
I have worked here for 5 years. (duration)
She has finished her homework. (completed with result)
They have visited Paris three times. (experience)
We have lived here since 2020. (unfinished time)
When to use: Past actions with present results, experiences, unfinished time periods (since/for).
üí° Memory tip: "Perfect" = completed action that's still relevant now!

4. Present Perfect Continuous

Advanced Duration
ü§î What's the formula combining Present Perfect + Continuous? Hint: "I have been working all morning"
Subject + have/has + been + Verb-ing
Examples:
I have been working all morning. (emphasis on duration)
She has been studying since 6 AM. (continuous action)
They have been waiting for an hour. (temporary situation)
It has been raining all day. (ongoing weather)
When to use: Actions that started in the past, continue to now, emphasizing duration or repetition.
üí° Memory tip: "Perfect Continuous" = ongoing action with connection to now!

üî¥ Past Tenses (5-8)

Actions that happened before now

5. Past Simple

Beginner Completed
ü§î How do we form Past Simple? Hint: "I worked yesterday"
Subject + Past Form of Verb (Regular: +ed, Irregular: 2nd form)
Examples:
I worked late yesterday. (completed action)
She studied abroad last year. (finished period)
They lived in Tokyo from 2018 to 2020. (past period)
We went to the cinema last weekend. (specific past time)
When to use: Completed actions in the past, specific past times, finished periods.
üí° Memory tip: "Simple Past" = one action, done and finished, like a closed book!

6. Past Continuous

Intermediate In Progress
ü§î What's the pattern for Past Continuous? Hint: "I was working when you called"
Subject + was/were + Verb-ing
Examples:
I was working when you called. (interrupted action)
She was studying at 9 PM last night. (specific past time)
They were living abroad while learning Spanish. (background)
It was raining all morning yesterday. (duration)
When to use: Actions in progress at specific past time, interrupted actions, background events.
üí° Memory tip: "Past Continuous" = like watching a movie of the past in action!

7. Past Perfect

Advanced Before Past
ü§î How do we show "past before past"? Hint: "I had finished work before the meeting started"
Subject + had + Past Participle (3rd form)
Examples:
I had finished work before the meeting started. (sequence)
She had studied English before moving to London. (preparation)
They had lived there for 5 years before moving. (duration before)
We had already eaten when they arrived. (completed before)
When to use: Actions completed before another past action, showing sequence in the past.
üí° Memory tip: "Past Perfect" = the "even earlier" past, like a flashback in a movie!

8. Past Perfect Continuous

Advanced Duration Before
ü§î How do we show ongoing action before past? Hint: "I had been working for 2 hours when you called"
Subject + had + been + Verb-ing
Examples:
I had been working for 2 hours when you called. (duration before)
She had been studying all day before the exam. (continuous effort)
They had been living there for years before buying the house. (long period)
It had been raining for hours before it stopped. (ongoing before)
When to use: Ongoing actions before another past point, emphasizing duration before past events.
üí° Memory tip: "Past Perfect Continuous" = showing how long something was happening before!

üü¢ Future Tenses (9-12)

Actions that will happen after now

9. Future Simple

Beginner Plans
ü§î How do we form Future Simple? Hint: "I will work tomorrow"
Subject + will + Base Verb
Examples:
I will work from home tomorrow. (future plan)
She will study abroad next year. (intention)
They will move to a new house soon. (decision)
It will rain tomorrow according to the forecast. (prediction)
When to use: Future plans, predictions, promises, spontaneous decisions, offers.
üí° Memory tip: "Will" = expressing your intention or what you think will happen!

10. Future Continuous

Intermediate In Progress
ü§î What's the pattern for Future Continuous? Hint: "I will be working at 3 PM tomorrow"
Subject + will + be + Verb-ing
Examples:
I will be working at 3 PM tomorrow. (specific future time)
She will be studying all weekend. (duration)
They will be traveling next month. (ongoing future)
We will be living there by next year. (future state)
When to use: Actions in progress at specific future time, ongoing future situations.
üí° Memory tip: "Future Continuous" = imagine yourself doing something at a future moment!

11. Future Perfect

Advanced Completion
ü§î How do we show completion by future time? Hint: "I will have finished by 5 PM"
Subject + will + have + Past Participle (3rd form)
Examples:
I will have finished work by 5 PM. (completion by deadline)
She will have graduated by next June. (future achievement)
They will have lived here for 10 years by 2025. (duration by future)
We will have completed the project by the deadline. (goal)
When to use: Actions that will be completed before a specific future time or event.
üí° Memory tip: "Future Perfect" = looking back from the future at what will be done!

12. Future Perfect Continuous

Advanced Duration
ü§î How do we show ongoing duration by future time? Hint: "I will have been working here for 5 years by December"
Subject + will + have + been + Verb-ing
Examples:
I will have been working here for 5 years by December. (duration milestone)
She will have been studying for 8 hours by evening. (continuous effort)
They will have been traveling for 24 hours when they arrive. (journey duration)
We will have been living together for a decade by our anniversary. (relationship milestone)
When to use: Emphasizing duration of ongoing actions up to a specific future point.
üí° Memory tip: "Future Perfect Continuous" = showing how long you'll have been doing something!
