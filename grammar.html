Parts of Speech

Understanding the different parts of speech is fundamental to mastering English grammar.

What are Parts of Speech?

Parts of speech are categories to which words are assigned based on their function in a sentence. The English language has eight main parts of speech:

Part of Speech Function Examples
Noun Names a person, place, thing, or idea John, London, table, happiness
Pronoun Replaces a noun I, you, he, she, it, they, we
Verb Shows action or state of being run, jump, think, is, were
Adjective Describes or modifies a noun happy, tall, beautiful, many
Adverb Modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb quickly, very, extremely
Preposition Shows relationship between words in, on, under, beside
Conjunction Connects words, phrases, or clauses and, but, or, because
Interjection Expresses emotion Wow!, Oh!, Ouch!
Example Sentence Analysis

The happy children played quietly in the beautiful park after school.

  • The - Article (type of adjective)
  • happy - Adjective (describes children)
  • children - Noun (the subject)
  • played - Verb (the action)
  • quietly - Adverb (describes how they played)
  • in - Preposition (shows relationship)
  • the - Article
  • beautiful - Adjective (describes park)
  • park - Noun (object of preposition)
  • after - Preposition
  • school - Noun (object of preposition)

Why Understanding Parts of Speech Matters

Knowing the parts of speech helps you:

  • Construct grammatically correct sentences
  • Understand the relationships between words
  • Improve your writing and speaking
  • Learn new vocabulary more effectively
  • Understand grammar rules and patterns
Common Mistakes
  • Confusing adjectives and adverbs: He drives quick vs. He drives quickly
  • Using nouns as verbs without proper changes: I will text to you tomorrow vs. I will text you tomorrow
  • Misplacing prepositions: What are you thinking about? vs. What are you thinking about? (Actually both are correct in modern English, but traditionally ending a sentence with a preposition was avoided)
Practice Exercise

Identify the parts of speech in the following sentence:

"Yesterday, my friendly neighbor carefully planted several colorful flowers in her garden."

Yesterday - Adverb (when)

my - Possessive adjective

friendly - Adjective (describes neighbor)

neighbor - Noun (subject)

carefully - Adverb (describes how)

planted - Verb (action)

several - Adjective (quantifies flowers)

colorful - Adjective (describes flowers)

flowers - Noun (direct object)

in - Preposition

her - Possessive adjective

garden - Noun (object of preposition)

Verb Tenses

Master the different verb tenses to express actions and states across past, present, and future time.

Understanding English Verb Tenses

English has 12 major verb tenses, formed by combining aspects (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous) with time references (past, present, future).

Simple Present Tense

Used for habits, general truths, and scheduled future events.

Subject + base form of verb (+ s/es for third person singular)
Examples

I work at a hospital.

She works at a school.

Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.

The train leaves at 6 PM tomorrow.

Present Continuous Tense

Used for actions happening now or temporary actions, and future arrangements.

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
Examples

I am studying for my exam right now.

She is working from home this week.

We are meeting the clients next Monday.

Simple Past
Subject + past form of verb (usually -ed)

I visited Japan last year.

She wrote a novel.

Past Continuous
Subject + was/were + verb-ing

I was reading when you called.

They were working all night.

Past Perfect
Subject + had + past participle

I had finished my work before dinner.

She had never seen snow before.

Common Tense Mistakes
  • Using present continuous with stative verbs: I am loving this movie vs. I love this movie
  • Incorrect past forms of irregular verbs: I goed to school vs. I went to school
  • Mixing tenses inappropriately: Yesterday I go to the store and bought milk vs. Yesterday I went to the store and bought milk

More Tenses and Their Uses

The complete overview of English tenses includes:

Tense Structure Main Uses
Present Perfect have/has + past participle Past actions with present relevance
Present Perfect Continuous have/has been + verb-ing Ongoing actions that started in past
Past Perfect Continuous had been + verb-ing Actions in progress before another past action
Simple Future will + base verb Predictions, spontaneous decisions
Future Continuous will be + verb-ing Actions in progress at a future time
Future Perfect will have + past participle Actions completed before a future time
Future Perfect Continuous will have been + verb-ing Ongoing actions up to a future time
Practice Exercise

Fill in the blanks with the correct verb forms:

1. By the time we arrive, the movie ________ (start) already.

2. I ________ (live) in this city for five years now.

3. She ________ (not finish) her homework yet.

4. While I ________ (cook), my friend called.

1. will have started (Future Perfect)

2. have been living (Present Perfect Continuous)

3. has not finished (Present Perfect)

4. was cooking (Past Continuous)
